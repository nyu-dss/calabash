MONGOOSES

I

Banded bodies on a termite mound
still as sculpted slate they look
with eyes as roundly inquisitive

as the child who stares back.
Rustle a paper bag, they will come
to you, expecting a treat.

Il

Oh sly, earthy ones
ochre-eyed and quick
as a winter sunset

when mother snake slides
malarial, from tree, you watch

flat, lade with quiet
I discover your secret.
